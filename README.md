# AI Engineering Journal

> Notes from building AI systems — the takeaways and what surprised me, not raw notes.

I'm an AI builder. This repo is the public layer: the parts worth keeping, written so someone else can skip the dead ends.

## Why this exists

Most AI content online is either marketing or fluff. The good stuff — what actually works in production, where the abstractions leak, what costs real money — gets locked inside companies. I'm trying to write the kind of thing I wish I'd found six months ago.

Each entry follows a tight format:

- **What I studied** — the source material (course, paper, doc, video)
- **The takeaway** — what I'd tell a colleague in 30 seconds
- **What surprised me** — the thing the official material undersells or skips
- **Code** — if applicable, working examples in `/code`

## Structure

```
week-XX/
├── README.md          # the takeaway
└── code/              # working examples (if applicable)
```
